September 20th

Tennis Bubbles

Twice a year the huge domes covering tennis courts need to be manipulated. I've never done this before. I found the job on Craigslist and then worked with a temp agency to get the details. I sent them my resume, provided my SIN, address, and birthdate over the phone.

I arrived on site at 8am. There were about 20 of us. The variety of people was interesting. Migrants, students, retirees, office workers, transient workers, return workers, and some people who were just loafing around.

I finished at 5pm, earning $22/hr + overtime.


Day 2

Much of the same today, but the fabric of the bubble was easier to work with. The skin in front of my knuckle has worn off from friction and I'll be wearing bandaids for a few days. If I do this again next year, and I hope I do, I'll tape up those digits with climbing tape to prevent that happening again.

Good experience. I have some thoughts on how to improve the process.

Thanks Kirk, Linden, and Lauren.
